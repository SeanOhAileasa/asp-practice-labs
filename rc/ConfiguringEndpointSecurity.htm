Task 1 - Update Windows Defender

Windows Defender is the built-in anti-malware software in Windows 10. It is essential that you keep this software updated with the latest signatures to keep malware at bay while using your computer.

To update Windows Defender, follow these steps:

Step 1

Ensure you have powered on the required devices and connect to PLABWIN10.

Click the Start charm to invoke the Start screen.

Step 2

From the Start screen, type the following:

defender

From the Search flyout menu, click Windows Defender and then press Enter.

Note: You may also see the Settings screen. If it appears, close it. You will require it later on.

Step 3

Click Close to any defender popups.

Click Scan now.

Note: On the Windows Defender screen, in the Virus and spyware definitions section, its signatures are outdated. The number of days since the definitions were created will differ in your lab environment. Therefore, this computer is at risk.

Step 4

On the Your PC is being scanned screen, Windows Defender starts the scan of the system for malware. The scanning may take a little while to complete.

Note: When you click Scan now, a quick scan is initiated, which is now being performed.

Step 5

After the scan is completed, click the Update tab.

Step 6

On the Update tab, click Update definitions.

Step 7

The update process starts and will take a few minutes to complete.

Step 8

When the update is completed, click Settings.

Step 9

From the Settings page, scroll to the Exclusions section.

Click Add an exclusion.

Step 10

On the Add an exclusion page, click Exclude a folder.

Navigate to the C Drive, select IEAK and then click Exclude this folder.

Step 11

This folder is now added to the exclusions.

Click the Back arrow button to return to Settings.

Step 12

You are back on the Settings page.

Click Open Windows Defender.

Step 13

On the Windows Defender window, you are now on the Home tab.

In the Scan options section, select Custom and then click Scan Now.

Step 14

The Windows Defender dialog box is displayed.

Expand Local Disk (C:), expand Windows and then select System32.

Click OK.

This will automatically begin the scan of that folder.

This scan will take a few minutes to complete.

Step 15

After the scan is finished, close Windows Defender and Settings windows.

Exercise 2 - Use Anti Spyware

An anti-spyware program is designed to catch spyware in your system, which cannot be caught with a usual antivirus program. Spyware is a malware that silently sits inside your system and collects information. There are different activities that spyware can perform, some of which are installing additional software without your knowledge or redirect your Web browser to an unwanted Website.

To get a better understanding of this technology, please refer to your course material or use your preferred search engine to research this topic in more detail.
